FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Which state should I form my LLC in? ▼
Most small business owners benefit from forming in the state where they actually do business. Delaware and Wyoming are popular for corporations and LLCs seeking flexibility and privacy. We can advise you during the ordering process.
How long does the filing process take? ▼
Standard processing is 5–7 business days. Expedited is 2–3 business days. Same-day service is available for orders placed before 12pm ET. Actual state processing time may vary.
What is a registered agent and do I need one? ▼
A registered agent is required in every state where your business is formed or registered. They receive official mail and legal notices on your behalf. You can serve as your own, but using a professional service adds privacy and ensures you never miss important documents.
Do state filing fees cost extra? ▼
Yes. State filing fees vary from around $50 to $500 depending on the state and entity type. Our service fee covers preparation, filing, and delivery. State fees are collected separately and we provide a full breakdown before processing.
Can I form a business if I'm not a US citizen? ▼
Yes! Foreign nationals can form LLCs and corporations in the United States. You generally do not need a Social Security Number. We assist with EIN applications for non-residents as well.
What's the difference between an LLC and a Corporation? ▼
LLCs offer flexible management and pass-through taxation, making them ideal for most small businesses. Corporations (C-Corp or S-Corp) are better suited for businesses seeking venture capital, issuing stock, or planning to go public. We can help you decide during the order process.
